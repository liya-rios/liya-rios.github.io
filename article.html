Climate news to watch in 2024

The world made major progress on climate change in 2023.

Here's what could come next.

by DANA NUCCIETELLI

JANUARY 3, 2024

GRID Alternatives employees install no-cost solar panels in California.(Photo by Mario Tama/Getty Images)

he past year broke a plethora of climate and energy records - some bad, but some good.

The year 2023 became the hottest year on record. Although climate scientists can't yet explain precisely why the year was so hot, long-term human-caused global warming and natural variability both played roles in breaking the record. And the past nine years have been the nine hottest on record.
